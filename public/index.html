CoNSTruct 2025

Build for Impact | Product x AI Hackathon

“Ideate. Prototype. Launch.”

Guiding document for participants · Nov 5 – Nov 30, 2025 · Hybrid · Bengaluru + Virtual

CoNSTruct 2025 is a month-long Build for Impact challenge co-created by Newton School of Technology, The Product Folks, and Emergent. We pair deliberate product craft with AI-enabled execution so every team has the runway to move from idea to a launch-ready MVP.

  • Product thinking - frame real problems and understand the user.
  • AI innovation - apply cutting-edge tools to ship smarter, faster.
  • Expert feedback & support - industry experts with you at each checkpoint.

Overview

The Build for Impact Hackathon is a month-long product + AI journey designed to move you from idea to MVP, with continuous guidance, masterclasses, and checkpoints.

Team Guidelines

Build as a Balanced Crew

Set the tone for collaboration and accountability from day one. These checkpoints keep every pod balanced and inclusive.

  • Team size capped at five students with at least one female builder.
  • Cross-batch collaboration is strongly encouraged to mix skills and context.
  • Keep a representative at every checkpoint so feedback loops stay warm.
Tools & Platforms

Ship with the Right Stack

Equip your pod with a focused toolkit so you can ideate, prototype, and launch without friction.

  • AI tooling: Emergent alongside your preferred IDE or builder workflow.
  • Product frameworks: Lean Canvas, JTBD, MVP roadmaps, and journey maps.
  • Collaboration: Emergent for rituals with Discord/Slack for async sync.

Hackathon Structure & Timeline

Two luminous phases keep your squad in flow. We alternate deep work, sonic breaks, and intimate guidance sessions so you never lose the cozy-but-electric momentum.

Phase 1 · Learning, Ideation & Prototyping

Week 1–2 · Nov 5 – Nov 19

  • Dates: Nov 5 – Nov 19
  • Kickoff Orientation & Icebreaker: introduction to the hackathon, format, and expectations.
  • Masterclasses:
    • Design Thinking & User Research
    • Prototyping & MVP Building
    • Product Positioning & Storytelling
    • Pitching & Go-to-Market Basics
  • Guided Sessions: product leaders and AI builders guide ideation and MVP creation.
  • Credits: every registered participant receives 50 credits for active participation from Emergent.
  • Checkpoint Submission: mid-week 2 progress deck plus early prototype demo.
  • Demo Day (End of Week 2):
    • Teams pitch their MVPs to judges.
    • Evaluation based on the official scoring rubric.
    • Top-performing teams unlock additional expert support for the Product Hunt launch sprint.

Phase 2 · Product Hunt Launch Sprint

Week 3–4 · Nov 20 – Dec 5

  • Dates: Nov 20 – Dec 5
  • Objective: validate real-world impact through Product Hunt launch and traction metrics.
  • Focus Areas:
    • Launch strategy & community building
    • Marketing collateral & product positioning
    • Tracking Product Hunt KPIs: upvotes, engagement, comments, feedback, and user traction
  • Expert Support: continued access to product experts for refining the live product and launch campaign.
  • Final Submission (End of Week 4):
    • Product Hunt launch summary
    • Product video
    • Updated prototype or product link
    • Post-launch performance metrics report
Top-performing teams unlock continued expert support, Product Hunt amplification, and community spotlights from Newton School of Technology, The Product Folks, and Emergent.

Team Guidelines & Support Stack

Set your crew up for success with clear expectations, recommended tools, and submission checklists.

Team Guidelines

Build as a Balanced Crew

  • Team size: max 5 students. One female student is mandatory.
  • Cross-batch collaboration is strongly encouraged.
  • At least one team member must attend every checkpoint.
Tools & Platforms

Ship with the Right Stack

  • AI tools: Emergent alongside any IDE of your choice.
  • Product frameworks: Lean Canvas, JTBD, MVP roadmaps, user journey maps.
  • Collaboration: Emergent for tracking, Slack/Discord for communication.
Submission Requirements

Checkpoint & Finale

  • Hackathon Submission (End of Week 2):
    • Working prototype - functional demo
    • Pitch deck - problem, solution, impact, roadmap
    • Demo video - 2–4 minutes
  • Final Submission (End of Week 4):
    • Product Hunt launch summary
    • Product video
    • Updated prototype or product link
    • Post-launch performance metrics report
Judging Criteria

How Teams Win

  • Product thinking – clarity of problem definition and user focus.
  • Innovation & creativity – originality of solution and use of AI.
  • Execution quality – technical soundness and MVP usability.
  • Progress journey – measurable improvement from start to finish.
  • Pitch & storytelling – clarity, confidence, and persuasiveness.
